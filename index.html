Welcome to Blackfish

Pacific Northwest, the way the Islands taught us.

Nestled into The Beach House at Rosario Harbor, Blackfish Café opens each morning to coffee, careful pastry, and the slow flicker of light across Cascade Bay. By midday the menu turns to bright, summer-leaning plates — the kind built for long lunches with a view.

Inspired by the natural beauty and community spirit of the San Juan Islands, we offer a casual, elevated space to gather, unwind, and savor the best of the Pacific Northwest.

— A new gathering place in Rosario Harbor.

Lunch on the Lawn

Picnic tables, sea breeze, and an unobstructed view.

When the sun is high we move outside — long picnic tables, broad umbrellas, and the kind of breeze that slows everything down. Cascade Bay opens wide in front of you, and lunch becomes a small ceremony.

It's casual, it's elevated, and it's one of the more pleasant ways to spend an afternoon in the San Juans.

The Beach House

Bay windows. Big history. The new hub of Rosario.

Blackfish lives inside The Beach House — comfortable, inviting indoor seating framed by giant windows that look straight onto the bay. Inside, it's warm and unhurried; outside, the patio runs warm right up to the water.

The Beach House has long been a gathering place at Rosario — by some accounts the largest event space in San Juan County — and it's now the heart of the resort's new chapter.
